About Nepal

Nepal, officially known as the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, is a landlocked country in South Asia. It is located mainly in the Himalayas, but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. Nepal is bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west.

Known as the birthplace of Lord Buddha and home to Mount Everest, the world's highest peak, Nepal is a country of incredible diversity. From the towering peaks of the Himalayas to the lush valleys and plains, Nepal offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and spiritual significance.

With a population of approximately 30 million people, Nepal is home to multiple ethnic groups, languages, and religions, making it a truly multicultural nation. The country's capital and largest city is Kathmandu, which serves as the political, cultural, and economic center of Nepal.

Why Visit Nepal?

Nepal offers something for everyone - from adventure seekers looking to trek in the Himalayas, to spiritual travelers seeking enlightenment in ancient temples and monasteries, to culture enthusiasts wanting to experience vibrant festivals and traditions. The warmth and hospitality of the Nepali people make every visit memorable.
